    \castingrange{\shortrange}
    \spelltarget{One living creature}
    \spellduration{Instantaneous}
    \savingthrow{Will netages; Spell Resist: yes}
    \spelldescription{You cause a living creature that is below 0 hit points but stabilized to resume dying. Upon casting this spell, you target a living creature that has -1 or fewer hit points.   \begin{spell}
    \spellname{Create Water}
    \spellschool{Conjuration (creation) [water]}
    \castingtime{\oneaction}
    \verbal\somatic
    \castingrange{\shortrange}
    \spelleffect{Up to 2 gallons of water/level}
    \spellduration{Instantaneous}
    \savingthrow{none; Spell Resist: no}
    \spelldescription{This spell generates wholesome, drinkable water, just like clean rain water. Water can be created in an area as small as will actually contain the liquid, or in an area three times as lange---possibly creating a downpour or filling many small receptacles. This water disappears after 1 day if not consumed. 
    
    Note: Conjuration spells can't create substances or objects within a creature. Water weighs about 8 pounds per gallon. One cubic foot of water contains roughly 8 gallons and weighs about 60 pounds.}
  \end{spell}
